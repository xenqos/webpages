Measures the weekly change in the number of barrels of commercial crude oil held by US firms.
The level of inventories influences the price of petroleum products, and have an impact on inflation.
Increase > than expected = weaker demand, bearish for crude prices. 
Decline in inventories < than expected = weaker demand, bearish for crude prices.
Increase < than expected = greater demand, bullish for crude prices. 
Decline in inventories > than expected = greater demand, bullish for crude prices.
